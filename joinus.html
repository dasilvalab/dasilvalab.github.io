Bioinformatics Research Scientist (São Paulo)

The Da Silva Lab is being established at the Instituto Israelita de Ensino e Pesquisa Albert Einstein (São Paulo, Brazil), led by Dr. Daniel da Silva (Assistant Professor, Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai).

We are seeking a Bioinformatics Research Scientist to join the group during this foundational stage and help build our new laboratory in Brazil. Our work integrates neuroscience, multi-omic data, and computational modeling to understand molecular and epigenetic mechanisms of vulnerability and resilience in psychiatric disorders.

Requirements

  • PhD and postdoctoral experience (including international experience)
  • Functional proficiency in English
  • Basic experience in bioinformatics and omics data analysis
  • Intermediate knowledge in R

Profile

A curious, collaborative scientist passionate about research and eager to grow scientifically and professionally.

Location: São Paulo, SP (Brazil)

How to apply:
Send your CV, a brief statement of research interests and contact information for 2 references to daniel.dasilva@mssm.edu . Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis.

Life in the Lab

Our lab combines rigorous neuroscience with a creative, data-driven mindset. We work collaboratively, value diverse perspectives, and believe that science thrives when curiosity meets purpose.

Located at Instituto Israelita de Ensino e Pesquisa Albert Einstein, our group benefits from outstanding core facilities and a vibrant neuroscience community spanning molecular, cellular, systems, and computational expertise.

Whether you enjoy patch-clamp electrophysiology, coding, imaging neurons, pharmacology or behavioral neuroscience, you’ll find space to grow here. We support independence, technical mastery, and teamwork equally.

We encourage scientists from underrepresented backgrounds to apply and join our mission to decode brain resilience.

Interested?
